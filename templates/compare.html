Track II Diplomacy

Back-channel Diplomacy
Definition Unofficial, problem-solving dialogue between non-state actors. Secret or discreet communication channels, which can involve official or unofficial actors.
Key Actors Academics, retired officials, civil society leaders, NGOs. Can be government officials, intelligence agents, or trusted intermediaries.
Primary Goal To generate new ideas, build trust, and reframe conflicts. To convey sensitive messages, test proposals, or negotiate secretly.
Formality Highly informal and structured around dialogue and workshops. Can be informal but often serves a direct, formal political purpose.
Example Oslo Accords (initial stages). U.S.-Iran nuclear talks via Omani intermediaries.
