Track II Diplomacy

Definition

Track II Diplomacy involves unofficial conflict resolution efforts by non-governmental actors. Unlike official Track I Diplomacy, it operates outside formal channels, allowing for exploration of new ideas without the political constraints of official negotiations.

Use in Practice

The most celebrated example is its role in facilitating the 1993 Oslo Accords between Israel and the PLO. Secret meetings hosted by Norwegian academics allowed for trust-building and a framework development that preceded the official agreement.

Theoretical Roots

Strongly rooted in Constructivist international relations theory, which emphasizes the role of non-state actors, norms, and shared identities in shaping global politics, challenging the state-centric focus of Realism.
